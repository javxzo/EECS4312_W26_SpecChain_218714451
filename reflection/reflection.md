# Application: [HeadSpace] 
- Most important difference between pipelines: [The automated pipeline achieved higher review coverage (0.1001) because it processed larger portions of the dataset automatically, while the manual pipeline focused on a smaller subset of reviews (0.0243) but allowed more deliberate interpretation of user feedback.]
- Most useful pipeline: [The hybrid pipeline produced the most useful outputs because it maintained the same completeness as the other pipelines (10 requirements and 20 tests with full traceability) while significantly reducing ambiguity (0.3), suggesting that combining automated generation with human refinement improved clarity.]
- Most surprising finding: [Despite differences in generation approach, all pipelines produced the same number of personas, requirements, tests, and traceability links, and all achieved a perfect traceability ratio (1.0) and testability rate (1.0). This suggests that structured prompts and templates can strongly constrain the outputs even when the generation method changes.]
- Observed weakness in the automated pipeline: [The automated pipeline had the highest ambiguity ratio (0.7), indicating that although it covered more reviews, the generated requirements tended to include more vague or imprecise wording that could reduce clarity without additional human review.]
